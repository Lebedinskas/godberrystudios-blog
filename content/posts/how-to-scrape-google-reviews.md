Google reviews are one of the most valuable sources of customer feedback on the internet. Whether you're doing competitor analysis, reputation monitoring, or market research, having structured access to review data gives you a serious edge.

The problem? Google doesn't offer a public API for reviews, and manually copying them is painfully slow. In this guide, I'll show you how to scrape Google reviews in under 60 seconds — no coding required.

## Why Scrape Google Reviews?

Businesses and researchers extract Google reviews for several reasons:

- **Reputation monitoring** — Track what customers say about your business over time
- **Competitor analysis** — Understand what customers love (and hate) about competitors
- **Market research** — Identify trends and common complaints across an industry
- **Sentiment analysis** — Feed reviews into AI tools for automated analysis
- **Lead generation** — Find businesses with poor reviews that might need your services
